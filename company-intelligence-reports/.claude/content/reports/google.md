# Alphabet (Google): Company Deep Dive

> GOOGL · NASDAQ · Internet & AI · Mountain View, CA · FY ends December 31

## Executive Summary

Alphabet reported **$402.8B** in revenue in FY2025, up **15.1%** year-over-year, with net income of **$132.2B** [1]. It employs roughly **190,000** people and runs the world's dominant search and digital-advertising franchise, which funds an aggressive, full-stack AI push [1][2]. That stack is unusual in its vertical integration: custom TPU silicon, the DeepMind research lab, the Gemini model family, and global distribution through Search, Android, Chrome, and YouTube [2]. Google Cloud has turned profitable and is now a third growth pillar alongside Search and YouTube [1]. The defining question is whether AI strengthens or cannibalizes the search economics that pay for everything else [2].

## Company Overview

Alphabet Inc. is the holding company for Google and Other Bets [1]. Google's core is Search, YouTube, advertising, Android, Chrome, and Google Cloud; Other Bets includes Waymo [1]. Founded in 1998 and headquartered in Mountain View, California, Alphabet has traded on NASDAQ since Google's 2004 IPO (GOOGL/GOOG) and employs about 190,000 staff [1]. It reports in three segments: Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets [1].

## Strategic Direction

### Full-Stack AI (Gemini + TPUs)

Alphabet builds the entire AI stack — custom TPU accelerators, DeepMind research, the Gemini model family, and consumer distribution [2]. Few competitors own silicon, frontier models, and a billion-user funnel at once [2].

- TPUs reduce dependence on third-party GPUs.
- Gemini is being woven into Search, Workspace, and Android.

### AI-Augmented Search and Cloud

Alphabet is reformatting Search around AI answers while protecting ad monetization, and scaling Google Cloud, which has reached sustained profitability [1][2]. Cloud is the clearest near-term AI revenue channel [1].

### Health and Scientific AI

Google is building healthcare-specific models — Med-Gemini for clinical reasoning and the open MedGemma suite — and an AI "co-scientist" for hypothesis generation, signaling investment in scientific AI [3].

## Hiring & Workforce Signals

Across ~190,000 employees, hiring and reorganization favor AI research and infrastructure, Google Cloud, and health/science AI, while core ads engineering is optimized for efficiency [1][3].

## Competitive Positioning

### Advantages

- **Search economics** — A dominant, high-margin ad franchise funds everything else [1][2].
- **Full-stack ownership** — Silicon, models, and distribution under one roof [2].
- **Distribution** — Search, Android, Chrome, and YouTube reach billions of users [2].

### Threats

- AI chat interfaces (ChatGPT and others) could disintermediate search queries [2].
- U.S. DOJ antitrust rulings target the search-default and ad-tech businesses [2].
- OpenAI and Anthropic compete for developer and enterprise AI mindshare [2].

### Market Share

Google holds the dominant share of global search and a leading position in digital advertising; Google Cloud is the #3 hyperscaler behind AWS and Azure [2].

## Key Risks

**Search disruption** — Generative AI could erode query volume or click-based monetization [2].

**Antitrust remedies** — DOJ cases on search distribution and ad tech could force structural or behavioral changes [2].

**AI capex** — Heavy infrastructure spending with uncertain payback timing [1].

**Regulatory breadth** — Global scrutiny spans privacy, competition, and content [1].

## Outlook

Alphabet enters the AI era from strength: $402.8B in revenue, $129.0B operating income, and the only fully vertically integrated AI stack at consumer scale [1][2]. The bull case is that Gemini and Cloud convert Alphabet's distribution and silicon into durable AI revenue; the bear case is search cannibalization plus antitrust remedies [2]. Watch Google Cloud growth and margin, Search resilience under AI answers, and the antitrust outcomes [1][2].
